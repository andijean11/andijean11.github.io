Sappho Fragments

Below are the three translated fragments #34, #94, and #96.



an honest death
it's all I want
                             she told me             left me
                             all this                     weeping
Oh me
        how awful
        we suffered
Sappho,
believe me--
I never willed to let you go
                                              (I answer to her)
Be well and gone
with my memory
you know how I worry

But if not, I want you still
to be reminded
        how beautifully
        we suffered

all those crowns of violets
                          of saffron
                          as one
                          you placed on me
all those garlands
                          woven round
                          soft necks
                          formed from flowers
                          you dropped
all that perfume
                          priced for a queen
                          you anointed
on soft beds
                          tender to touch
                          your longing loosed

no one               no thing                  was
                          no shrine
                          no grove
                          no dance
                          no sound












in Sardis now
here
so often holding
in her mind
the image of a goddess
(you)

standing out in Lydia
like a bloom-fingered moon
at the sun's dipping
surpassing all                 stars       about a full moon
                                                    hiding away
holding her light over                   a shining form

salt seas
maybe even                                  what ever
flowered fields                             filled earth
                                                    with so much
                                                    light?

pouring the dew (and it pours pretty)
where roses grow       and tender chervils
and bloom-scent melilot
even in her wandering
she's reminded of you, mild Attis
anywhere              we were not      somewhere
                                                      slight of mind
                                                      heart-fed
                                                      her longing lasts
