About this Project


This website was made possible by the University of Iowa Libraries Digital Scholarship & Publishing Studio, and their Summer Fellowship Program, supported by the University of Iowa Graduate College and the Studio Steering Committee. The fellowship allowed nine graduate students to work on "digital publishing" in whatever form it took as it applied to their field of study over an eight week period in the summer of 2017.

My idea was to generate a new type of translation website that did more than show the original and the translation side-by-side. With digital media, "readers" can become "users," actively involved in the reading experience. Translation is a series of complicated decisions, and I invite you to explore the decision processes I go through when I translate from French. This exploration includes examining the usefulness and the limits of Machine Translation (namely Google Translate).

In addition, I hope to create a space where translations can move beyond pen and paper. I have featured three poems by Laura Moser whose ideas are enhanced by digital features.

Finally, I invite you to read through my blog, which relies on the use of images to communicate my thoughts as I work on this project.

Check out the Digital Scholarship Publishing Studio on their website and on twitter.

About Andrea


Andrea received her Bachelor of Arts in English and French with a European Studies Concentration from Grinnell College in 2015. There, she was both a French tutor and lab assistant. She also was a research assistant on a project involving Wagner's influence on the music culture of late nineteenth century France. During her junior year, she studied abroad in Nantes, France with the IES program. During the 2015-2016 academic year, she served for Americorps in North Minneapolis teaching literacy skills to at-risk third graders. She is currently an MFA student in Literary Translation; she translates from French to English, primarily from Francophone Caribbean literature. She also teaches for the Department of French at the elementary and intermediate levels. When she isn't reading, she is playing her cello, trying a new recipe, playing video games, or planning her next vacation.
