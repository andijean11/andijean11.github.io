Sappho Fragments

Imagine finding a wealth of Sappho's texts, but only in fragments, half-erased by the years. How would you reconstitute these into three "full" poems? Drag words, verses, and stanzas to explore the themes present in Sappho's work.

an honest death
it's all I want

she told me
all this

left me
weeping

Oh me

how awful
we suffered

Sappho,
believe me--
I never willed to let you go

(I answer to her)

Be well and gone
with my memory
you know how I worry

But if not, I want you still
to be reminded
how beautifully
we suffered

in Sardis now
here
so often holding
in her mind
the image of a goddess

(you)

all those crowns

all those garlands

all that perfume

of violets
of saffron
as one
you placed on me

woven round
soft necks
formed from flowers
you dropped

priced for a queen
you anointed
on soft beds
tender to touch
your longing loosed

standing out in Lydia
like a bloom-fingered moon
at the sun's dipping
surpassing all

stars

about a full moon
hiding away
a shining form

holding her light over

salt seas
maybe even
flowered fields

what ever
filled earth
with so much

light?

pouring the dew
(and it pours pretty)
where roses grow
and tender chervils

and bloom-scent melilot

even in her wandering
she's reminded
of you, mild Attis

somewhere
slight of mind
heart-fed
her longing lasts

no one no thing

was anywhere

no shrine
no grove
no dance
no sound
